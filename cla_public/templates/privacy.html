{{ _('Civil Legal Advice (“CLA”) - Legal Aid Agency Privacy Notice ') }}

{{ _('Purpose') }}

{% trans %}This privacy notice sets out the standards that you can expect from the Legal Aid Agency when we request or hold personal information (‘personal data’) about you; how you can get access to a copy of your personal data; and what you can do if you think the standards are not being met.{% endtrans %}

{{ _('The Legal Aid Agency is an Executive Agency of the Ministry of Justice (MoJ). The MoJ is the data controller for the personal information we hold. The Legal Aid Agency collects and processes personal data for the exercise of its own and associated public functions. Our public function is to provide legal aid.') }}

{% trans %}The CLA Operator Service is delivered by Hinduja Global Solutions UK Ltd, a contractor employed by the Legal Aid Agency to deliver CLA. If it is determined that you qualify for CLA, we may put you through to one of a number of CLA Specialist Advice Providers. These organisations are data processors on behalf of the Legal Aid Agency and Ministry of Justice.{% endtrans %}

{{ _('About personal information') }}

{% trans %}Personal data is information about you as an individual. It can be your name, address or telephone number.{% endtrans %}

{% trans %}The kind of information that CLA will collect about you includes:{% endtrans %}

  • {% trans %}Personal details including your name, address, date of birth, contact details, diversity monitoring information and national insurance number;{% endtrans %}
  • {{ _('Financial information needed to determine if you qualify for legal aid;') }}
  • {{ _('Reason for contacting CLA, such as the nature of your problem or specific details about your situation;') }}
  • {{ _('Details of any specific needs that you may have in order to access the CLA service; and') }}
  • {% trans %}Responses to any customer satisfaction survey or details of any feedback, complaints or requests for a review of our decision about whether you qualify for legal aid.{% endtrans %}

{% trans %}We will only save the information you enter if you decide to contact a Civil Legal Advice operator. Therefore, the personal information that you have submitted, will only be stored when you click ‘submit your application’.{% endtrans %}

{% trans %}We know how important it is to protect customers’ privacy and to comply with data protection laws. We will safeguard your personal data and will only disclose it where it is lawful to do so, or with your consent.{% endtrans %}

{{ _('Types of personal data we process') }}

{% trans %}We only process personal data that is relevant for the services we are providing to you. The personal data which you have provided will only be used for the purposes set out below.{% endtrans %}

{{ _('Purpose of processing and the lawful basis for the process') }}

{% trans %}The purpose of the Legal Aid Agency collecting and processing the personal data which you have provided on this form is for the purposes of providing legal aid. Specifically, we will use this personal data in the following ways:{% endtrans %}

  • {% trans %}In deciding whether you qualify for legal aid. This is to assess whether your issue is within the scope of the CLA and whether you are financially eligible.{% endtrans %}
  • {% trans %}In assisting us in providing information and/or advice services to you.{% endtrans %}
  • {% trans %}To prevent or detect crime and fraud.{% endtrans %}
  • {% trans %}In conducting periodic assurance audits on legal aid files to ensure that decisions have been made correctly and accurately.{% endtrans %}
  • {% trans %}In producing statistics and information on our processes to enable us to improve and monitor our processes to assist us in carrying out our functions. This information is collated in an anonymous format, it will not be used to identify you.{% endtrans %}

{% trans %}Were the Legal Aid Agency unable to collect this personal information, we would not be able to conduct the activities above, which would prevent our ability to provide legal aid.{% endtrans %}

{% trans %}The lawful basis for the Legal Aid Agency collecting and processing your personal data is for the administration of justice and the result of the powers contained in Legal Aid, Sentencing and Punishment of Offenders Act 2012.{% endtrans %}

{% trans %}We also collect ‘special categories of personal data’ for the purposes of monitoring equality, this is a legal requirement for public authorities under the Equality Act 2010. Special categories of personal data obtained for equality monitoring will be treated with the strictest confidence and any information published will not identify you or anyone else associated with your legal aid application.{% endtrans %}

  • {% trans %}Non-public authorities such as: Credit reference agencies Equifax and Transunion and our debt collection partners Marston Holdings;{% endtrans %}
  • {% trans %}Hinduja Global Solutions UK Ltd and{% endtrans %}
  • {% trans %}If false or inaccurate information is provided or fraud identified, the Legal Aid Agency can lawfully share your personal information with fraud prevention agencies to detect and to prevent fraud and money laundering.{% endtrans %}

{% trans %}You can contact our Data Protection Officer for further information on the organisations we may share your personal information with.{% endtrans %}

{% trans %}Any personal data shared with a data processor for this purpose will be governed by model contract clauses under data protection law. {% endtrans %}

{{ _('Details of transfers to third country and safeguards') }}
