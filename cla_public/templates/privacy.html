{{ _('How is my privacy protected?') }}

{% trans %}Protecting your privacy is important to us. You can use this service to find out if you might qualify for legal aid without providing any personal information that could identify you.{% endtrans %}

{% trans %}We only save the information you enter if you decide to contact a Civil Legal Advice operator.{% endtrans %}

{% trans %}At this point, you will need to tell us your name and phone number and all the information you’ve entered will be stored when you click ‘Submit your application’. We store this information so that the operator can see it when they speak to you.{% endtrans %}

{% trans %}If you don’t want to speak to an operator, you don’t need to enter any personal details and we don’t store any of the information you have entered.{% endtrans %}

{% trans %}To provide you with the best service that we can, Civil Legal Advice may share some information about you with other organisations. But we will only do this, if you have given us permission or when we have a lawful basis for doing so.{% endtrans %}

{% trans %}In exceptional circumstances, we may share limited information about you without your consent. This would only be to prevent fraud, to help detect crime, if it is required by law or by a court order or if we are very worried about your safety or the safety of others.{% endtrans %}

{% trans %}In order to provide a service to you, Civil Legal Advice (CLA) may need to view, collect, record and hold (‘process’) personal data about you.{% endtrans %}

{% trans %}Personal data is information about you as an individual. It can be your name, address or telephone number. It can also include the information that you have provided in this form, such as your financial circumstances.{% endtrans %}

{% trans %}This statement explains how CLA will process any personal data that we collect from you or that you provide to us.{% endtrans %}

{% trans %}The Legal Aid Agency (LAA) is responsible for CLA service and the Ministry of Justice (MoJ) is the Data Controller. The Legal Aid Agency (LAA) is an Executive Agency of the MoJ.{% endtrans %}

{% trans %}The LAA works with other organisations who deliver the CLA service. For example, when you first make contact with CLA, you will communicate with the CLA Operator Service, which is delivered by Hinduja Global Solutions UK Limited (a contractor employed by the LAA).{% endtrans %}

{% trans %}Once you have contacted the CLA Operator Service, if you are likely to qualify for Legal Aid, the operator will transfer you to a CLA Specialist Provider. If you do not qualify to receive CLA Specialist advice, the operator will inform you accordingly and direct you to alternative sources of help.{% endtrans %}

{% trans %}The CLA service is committed to following this Privacy Statement. CLA Specialists must also adhere to their own internal privacy policy.{% endtrans %}

{{ _('What types of personal data do we process?') }}

{% trans %}CLA will only collect the minimum amount of information that is necessary and relevant, in order to deliver the CLA service to you.{% endtrans %}

{{ _('The kind of information CLA may collect about you includes:') }}

  • {% trans %}Personal details including your name, address, date of birth, contact details, diversity monitoring information and national insurance number;{% endtrans %}
  • {{ _('Financial information needed to determine if you qualify for legal aid;') }}
  • {{ _('Reason for contacting CLA, such as the nature of your problem or specific details about your situation;') }}
  • {{ _('Details of any specific needs that you may have in order to access the CLA service; and') }}
  • {% trans %}Responses to any customer satisfaction survey or details of any feedback, complaints or requests for a review of our decision about whether you qualify for legal aid.{% endtrans %}

{% trans %}The amount of information CLA will collect will depend on whether you qualify for the CLA service and/or your specific needs. For example, you will be asked to provide more information before we can transfer you through to a CLA Specialist.{% endtrans %}

{{ _('How does CLA use your data and why?') }}

CLA {{ _('collects and records information about you to:') }}

  • {{ _('Work out whether you qualify for legal aid;') }}
  • {{ _('Provide information and/or advice services to you;') }}
  • {{ _('Save you from repeating information every time you contact us;') }}
  • {{ _('Help us monitor and improve our service;') }}
  • {% trans %}Build statistics about the CLA service, monitor fraud and justify public expenditure. This information is collated in an anonymous format;{% endtrans %}
  • {% trans %}Collect views on the CLA service and any improvements considered necessary. Where consent is provided, CLA User contact details may be passed onto an independent research agency;{% endtrans %}
  • {{ _('Prevent or detect crime or fraud; and') }}
  • {{ _('Conduct occasional assurance audits to ensure that the decisions we make are correct and accurate.') }}

{% trans %}Without this information, CLA would not be able to conduct the activities above which are essential to the delivery of legal aid.{% endtrans %}

{% trans %}The lawful basis for CLA collecting and processing your personal data is the result of the powers contained in legislation, specifically the Legal Aid, Sentencing and Punishment of Offenders Act 2012.{% endtrans %}

{% trans %}CLA also collects ‘special categories of personal data’ for the purposes of monitoring equality. This is a legal requirement for public authorities, under the Equality Act 2010. Special categories of personal data obtained for equality monitoring will be treated with the strictest confidence and any information published will not identify you or anyone else associated with your legal aid application.{% endtrans %}

{% trans %}Given the purposes for which your personal data may be used (as referred to above) it is essential that the information you provide is accurate and complete.{% endtrans %}

{{ _('How does CLA collect your personal data and keep it safe?') }}

{% trans %}CLA may collect personal data about you by phone, online, email or post (see table below). Any personal data will be recorded and stored within a secure system that meets required Government standards.{% endtrans %}

{% trans %}CLA will be able to view and verify the information that you provide and, where necessary, will amend it to maintain its accuracy.{% endtrans %}

{% trans %}The minimum amount of personal data that CLA will ask to collect is your name and two other pieces of personal information that could be used to identify you, e.g. postcode, date of birth or telephone number.{% endtrans %}

{% trans %}As your personal data is stored on our IT infrastructure, and shared with our data processors, it may be transferred and stored securely outside the European Economic Area (EEA). Where that is the case, it will be subject to equivalent legal protection through the use of Model Contract Clauses.{% endtrans %}

{% trans %}Any transfers made will be in full compliance with all aspects of the data protection law.{% endtrans %}

{% trans %}By submitting your personal data, you agree to information being recorded, used and stored as outlined in this Privacy Statement.{% endtrans %}
